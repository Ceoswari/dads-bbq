🥩 Beef & Lamb doneness

Pull a few degrees early — temperature keeps climbing while it rests (carryover). These are the final temps after resting.

🌡️ Pull temps by cut
😴 How long to rest
CutRest timeWhy
Steaks, chops5–10 minJuices settle so they don't run out when you cut.
Roasts, whole birds20–30 minBigger mass, more carryover — tent loosely with foil.
Brisket, big roasts1–2 hoursWrap in towels in a dry cooler; stays hot and gets more tender.

🌡️ A good instant-read thermometer is your single best tool. Poultry has hard safety minimums (165°F); for whole-muscle beef and lamb, doneness is your call — cook to the temp you like.

Match the wood to the meat — and remember the cardinal rule: you want thin, almost-invisible blue smoke, never thick white billows. Less is more.

💡 Mix woods to build your own flavor — a base of oak with a little cherry is a classic all-rounder that works on almost anything.
